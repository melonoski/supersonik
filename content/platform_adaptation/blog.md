# Blog / Long-Form Format Rules

Voice from `../voices/<person>_voice.md` stays the same. The format adapts to long-form web content.

## General

- **More room to be detailed and technical.** Blog readers chose to click — you don't have to earn every line the way you do on LinkedIn.
- **Subheads matter.** Readers scan first, read second. Use H2 every 200–400 words.
- **Include real numbers, real examples, real screenshots** where possible. Source every stat.
- **Your voice can relax here.** Long-form lets you breathe.

## Structure

- **Hook (first 1–2 paragraphs):** Same rule as LinkedIn — anchor in something specific, no "In today's..." opens.
- **Body:** Mix narrative paragraphs with bullet lists. Don't go more than ~5 paragraphs without a subhead or list to break the wall.
- **Conclusion:** Single takeaway + one specific next action for the reader (try it, read the next post, DM you). No power-line summary.

## Length guidance

| Post type | Target length |
|---|---|
| Pain-point SEO | 1,200–1,800 words |
| Thought-leadership | 1,000–1,500 words |
| Comparison / "X vs Y" | 1,500–2,500 words |
| Customer story | 600–1,000 words |
| Tutorial / how-to | 1,500–3,000 words |

## Citations + links

- **Cite every category benchmark inline.** Source URL in parentheses or as a hyperlink on the stat.
- **Internal links:** every Supersonik claim should link to the most relevant page on the site.
- **External links:** OK in body for blog (unlike LinkedIn). Open in new tab.

## SEO basics

- Title: keyword + benefit, under 60 chars.
- Meta description: 140–160 chars, includes the primary keyword once.
- H1 matches the title. Only one H1 per post.
- First 100 words contain the primary keyword naturally.

## Anti-patterns (from `global.md`)

Read `global.md` first. All bans apply.

Blog-specific extras:
- ❌ "Five tips for X" listicles without a unique angle.
- ❌ Walls of text without subheads.
- ❌ Unsourced stats. If you can't link the source, leave the stat out.
- ❌ Conclusion that just restates the intro.
