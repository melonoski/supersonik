# LinkedIn Format Rules

Voice from `../voices/<person>_voice.md` stays the same. The format adapts to LinkedIn.

## LinkedIn Posts

- **Short paragraphs.** 1–2 sentences max per block.
- **White space is your friend.** The feed is dense — your post needs to breathe.
- **Hook in the first line.** Not clickbait — a real hook that earns the next line.
- **No links in the post body.** LinkedIn's algorithm buries posts with outbound links. Put links in the first comment.
- **Personal experience beats abstract advice.** "I did X and learned Y" outperforms "5 tips for Z" every time.
- **Hashtags:** 1–2 max. Never a cluster at the end.
- **Length:** under 200 words unless it's a milestone story (launch, acquisition, fundraise, major thought-leadership piece).

## LinkedIn DMs

- **First line is the hook.** Same rule as posts — a specific reference to the recipient (signal, post they wrote, company action).
- **Under 75 words.** DMs longer than that get bounced.
- **No greeting filler.** Skip "Hope this finds you well" — start with the anchor.
- **One ask per DM.** Same as email.
- **No links in the first DM.** Save them for the second message after a reply.

## Engagement boosters (empirically observed)

These come from voice-file analytics — apply per voice:

- **Naming people by name** → increases comments.
- **Image attached** → 6×+ lift on likes, 13×+ on comments vs text-only.
- **Country flags in the hook** → raises top-of-feed stop rate.
- **Numbers in the first 2 sentences** → stops the scroll, builds credibility.
- **Open-ended question at the close** → drives comments.

## Anti-patterns (from `global.md`)

Read `global.md` first. Don't repeat the ban list here — it applies everywhere.

LinkedIn-specific extras:
- Don't end every post with a one-sentence power line (most detectable AI pattern on LinkedIn).
- Don't reuse the same opening hook structure twice in a row from the same author.
