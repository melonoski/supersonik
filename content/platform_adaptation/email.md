# Email Format Rules

Voice from `../voices/<person>_voice.md` stays the same. The format adapts to outbound email.

## Cold outbound

- **One ask per email.** Not two. Not "and also." One.
- **Under 100 words.** Anything longer gets skimmed.
- **Sound like a person, not a company.** First person singular: "I", not "we".
- **Reference something specific about them.** Not "I saw your company is growing" — generic. "I saw you hired 3 AEs in the last month" — specific.
- **Subject line:** 4–7 words. Lowercase preferred. No clickbait. No questions you immediately answer.

## Warm outbound (after engagement / referral / prior touch)

- **Under 150 words.** A little more room — they already know you.
- **Reference the prior touch** in the first line. Acknowledge what they did/said.
- **One ask, still.** No multi-CTAs even when warm.

## Reply emails (after they responded)

- **Mirror their length.** If they sent 3 sentences, send 3.
- **Drop the formality.** Match their register.
- **Keep moving the conversation forward** — propose one next step.

## Signal-anchored opens (the right shape)

| Signal type | Open line shape |
|---|---|
| Series A/B/C in last 90 days | "You announced your Series B last month. The first thing every Series B SaaS underestimates is..." |
| Hiring SE/Demo Engineer roles | "Your careers page has 4 SE openings. Two of them are in Spanish-speaking markets." |
| Demo form on homepage | "Your homepage demo button still routes through a 4-step form. [Category benchmark, cited]." |
| Competitor stack (Walnut/Storylane/Reprise) | Open with the specific competitor displacement angle. |
| New CRO/VP Sales <6mo tenure | "Saw you started as CRO at [Company] in [month]. Curious what's at the top of your pipeline math right now." |

## Anti-patterns (from `global.md`)

Read `global.md` first. All bans apply.

Email-specific extras:
- ❌ "Hope this finds you well" / "Quick question" / "Following up" — never.
- ❌ Multiple CTAs.
- ❌ Generic value-prop dump before establishing the anchor signal.
- ❌ Cold email longer than 100 words.
