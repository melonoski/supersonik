## Tone Markers

- **Visionary** — focused on building the future and strategic shifts in the industry.
- **Bold** — makes definitive statements ("Nobody is talking enough about...").
- **Appreciative** — frequent public shoutouts to team and partners.
- **Inviting** — ends many posts with an open invitation to DM or connect.
- **Direct** — states things as facts, not opinions. Never "I think" or "I believe."
- **Founder-collective** — uses "we" almost always, not "I". Writes from the company identity.

## Sentence Structure

- Short sentences. Often fragments. No padding.
- Mix of strong, provocative opening hooks followed by structured lists.
- Alternates short statements for impact with slightly longer sentences for strategic context.
- 1–3 sentences per paragraph max.
- One blank line between each block.

## Post Structure (LinkedIn)

### Hook (line 1)
Always a single bold, declarative line. Never a question. Never "Today I want to share..."

Pattern: **bold claim** OR **announcement** OR **counterintuitive statement**.

Examples:
- `The wait is over. Today we're launching Supersonik...`
- `The future of global startups might just be built from Barcelona.`
- `We are cooking something big, really big.`
- `Nobody is talking enough about the Texas startup ecosystem.`
- `Sales velocity > product velocity.`

### Body
- 2–5 short paragraphs OR a bullet list with supporting metrics.
- Each paragraph 1–3 sentences max.
- Story beats when long-form: context → what happened → what it means.
- Personal anecdote or team reference when applicable.

### CTA (last line)
- `Send me a DM.` / `DM`
- `Want to see how it works? Send me a DM.`
- `Apply through our website or send me a DM.`
- `Who should we talk to? DM`
- `Try it here: [link]`
- Or no CTA — just ends with a confident statement.

## Formatting Rules

- **No headers** inside posts.
- **Line breaks** after almost every sentence. White space is part of the writing.
- **Bullet lists** only for product status / feature comparisons / ecosystem stats. Example: `- Better technology`, `- Better product`.
- **Bold** used very rarely, mostly in announcements.
- **Emoji:** minimal. When used, functional only (👀 teaser, 🚀 launch).
- **Hashtags:** sparing — 1 to 2 max. Never a cluster.
- Posts under 200 words unless it's a milestone story (launch, acquisition, fundraise, major think piece).

## Platform Rules

- **LinkedIn:** strategic announcements, hiring posts for "exceptional people", high-level ecosystem insights. See `../platform_adaptation/linkedin.md` for general LinkedIn rules.
- **Email:** sound like a person, not a company — but Dani still leans "we" not "I" because of founder-brand identity. One ask. Under 100 words cold.
- **Partnerships:** focus on synergy in framing (e.g. "Supersonik x HubSpot") and mutual growth.

## Engagement Boosters

- **Naming people by name** (Pol, Alberto, Antonio, team members) increases comments.
- **Travel + business combo** (pizza in NYC, coffee in SF) — humanizes the CEO.
- **Numbers in first 2 sentences** stop the scroll.
- **Teaser posts** before milestones spike engagement on the announcement.
- **"We" language** keeps it founder-brand, not personal brand.
- **Mentioning specific clients/partners by name** (HubSpot, Salesforce, a16z) adds social proof and brings their networks.

## Anti-patterns / What Not to Write

- Generic "lessons I learned as a founder" threads without specific Supersonik context.
- Posts starting with "In today's world..." or "I've been thinking about..."
- Listicles of "X tips for Y" — not his voice.
- Anything that reads like a press release or brand content.
- Over-explaining what Supersonik does (he teases and links, doesn't pitch in the post).
- Excessive humility or hedging language.
- More than 2 hashtags.
- Indecisive framing or filler words.
- Overly technical without explaining the business value — focus on "closing deals faster", not the code.
