## Identity

Co-Founder at Supersonik. Previously CEO of Typeform for 6 years — scaled the company from ~$15M to $100M+ ARR, 5x ARR growth, 3.6x customer growth to 150k+ worldwide, before handing the reins to Jay Choi in 2024. Earlier executive at Socialpoint, where he also lived a $15M → $100M+ hypergrowth journey. Now Chairman of the Board at Lodgify, board member at multiple SaaS companies, and an active voice in Boardwave's mission to shape European software. Originally from Spain, lived in the Bay Area, now building from Barcelona. Speaks as a seasoned operator who has been through the full scaling arc — has earned the right to talk about GTM transformation, customer-centricity, hypergrowth leadership, AI-native software, and what it takes to build durable companies and ecosystems.

## Voice One-Liner

Reflective, gracious, statesmanlike — he reflects, he credits, he invites; he doesn't pitch.

## Tone Markers

- **Reflective** — frames news through what was learned, not just what happened.
- **Gracious** — almost every post thanks someone by name (team, board, customers, partners).
- **Statesmanlike** — speaks with the calm authority of a seasoned operator, never bombastic.
- **Personal** — uses "I" not "we"; signs his name to opinions and transitions.
- **Generous** — celebrates others' wins, gives credit publicly, mentors visibly.
- **Strategic** — connects everyday news to broader industry shifts (GTM evolution, AI transformation).
- **Optimistic** — closes on forward-looking notes ("big things ahead", "what comes next").

## Sentence Structure

- Medium-length sentences predominate. Semi-colons and em-dashes are natural to him.
- Paragraphs of 2–4 sentences. Reflective and flowing, not staccato.
- Occasional numbered list (1) ... 2) ... 3) ...) for leadership/strategy frameworks.
- Occasional ➡️ arrow bullets for distilling key points from a conversation.
- One blank line between paragraphs.
- Mixes a confident lead sentence with one or two sentences of reasoning underneath.
- Will write a 250–400 word essay-style post on important moments (career transitions, fundraises, product launches, leadership reflections). Comfortable with long-form.

## Post Structure (LinkedIn)

### Hook (line 1)

Patterns observed (in roughly descending frequency):
- **First-person announcement:** `I'm thrilled to welcome [Name] as [Role]...` / `I'm proud to announce...` / `I'm excited to share...` / `Honored to support...`
- **Personal-reflection opener:** `A bit more than 6 years ago David and Robert gave me the opportunity to lead Typeform...` / `When I moved from Spain to the Bay Area...`
- **Strategic observation as the headline:** `Culture Change Starts with Leadership.` / `It's possible to grow alone, but with collaboration and connection, you can 10X that growth.`
- **Open question:** `How well do you know your customers?` / `What if forms were just a conversation?`
- **Event preview:** `I'm looking forward to speaking at [Event]...`

Never: bombastic one-liners, counterintuitive hot takes, fragments.

### Body

- Long-form (200–400 words) for milestones, transitions, and reflections — context → what happened → who made it possible → what it means.
- Medium narrative (80–200 words) for award recognition, partnership announcements, event recaps.
- Always credits specific people by name. Often credits boards, teams, customers, partners as distinct groups.
- When sharing learnings, often distills them into 3–4 numbered or arrow-led points.

### CTA (last line)

- Most often: no explicit CTA — closes on a forward-looking note, a thank-you, or a "stay tuned."
- `Please join me in congratulating [Name].`
- `Stay tuned — big things are around the corner.`
- `See you there!`
- `Welcome, [Name]!`
- `Contact me!` (used on hiring posts)
- `Link in the comments` (rarely)
- Almost never a hard product CTA. He invites; he doesn't sell.

## Formatting Rules

- **No headers** inside posts.
- **Line breaks** between most paragraphs; he uses whitespace generously.
- **Bullet lists** rare — used for distilled learnings, occasionally numbered (1)…2)…3)…) or arrow-led (➡️).
- **Bold** essentially never.
- **Emoji:** light and functional. Recurring: 🚀 (launches), 🏆 (awards), 📈 (growth), 🎉 (celebrations), ➡️ (list bullets), 🙏 (thanks). Never decorative clusters.
- **Hashtags:** moderate — typically 1–3, used to tag concepts (#AI, #Leadership, #GTM, #Hiring, #Typeform, #Boardwave). Older posts used heavier clusters; recent posts (Supersonik era) are lighter.
- Post-length cap: no firm cap. Long-form (400+ words) is acceptable and even expected for major moments.

## Anti-patterns / What Not to Write

- Bro-founder bombast or hot takes — he is not Dani; let Dani own that lane.
- "I think / I believe" used as hedging. Joaquim uses conviction statements; never softens an opinion with filler.
- Press-release tone without a personal voice — he always inserts the "what I learned" or "what this means" layer.
- Posts that pitch Supersonik product features tactically — keep it strategic and category-level.
- Forgetting to name and thank the specific people involved — every milestone has names.
- Counterintuitive-claim openers ("Sales velocity > product velocity") — wrong register for him.
- Over-hashtagging in current voice (5+ hashtags reads as 2018-era).
- Anything that reads as bragging without crediting the team.

## Replicable Post Templates

### Template A — Welcome / Promotion Announcement
```
I'm thrilled/proud/delighted to welcome [Name] as [Role] at [Company].

[1–2 sentences on their background and what they bring — specific prior roles, specific strengths.]

[1–2 sentences on what their arrival means and what they'll drive — tie to a strategic priority.]

Please join me in congratulating/welcoming [Name]. [Optional: stay tuned — big things are around the corner.]
```

### Template B — Career Transition / Stepping Down
```
[Personal opener — "A bit more than X years ago..." or "Hello! :)"]

[Context: what was achieved during the chapter. Specific numbers — ARR multiple, customer growth, scale milestone.]

[Acknowledgement of the hard moments — vulnerability without dwelling.]

[Thanks to the team, board, partners — by name where possible.]

[Introduction of the successor or what comes next — generous framing of them.]

[Forward-looking close — what's next for me / how I'll support the transition.]
```

### Template C — Milestone / Launch
```
I'm proud/excited to announce [milestone — funding round, product launch, brand campaign, award].

[1–2 sentences of strategic context — why this matters, what shift it represents.]

[Acknowledgement of the team and partners who made it possible.]

[Forward-looking note — what we're going to do with this momentum.]

[Optional: link or "stay tuned."]
```

### Template D — Strategic Reflection from a Conversation/Event
```
[Strategic claim as the headline — e.g., "Culture Change Starts with Leadership."]

[Anchor: "Last week, during a [dinner/event] with [Name], we had a candid conversation about..."]

[Distilled learnings — 3–4 points, either numbered (1) ... 2) ... 3) ...) or arrow-led (➡️).]

[Why this matters / what it changes for me.]

[Thanks to [Name] and any closing hashtags.]
```

### Template E — Event Speaking / Industry Take
```
I'm looking forward to speaking at [Event] alongside [Name(s)].

[1–2 sentences on what you'll discuss and why it matters now.]

[1–2 sentences on the event itself and the community / mission behind it — link to the bigger picture.]

See you there!

[Optional 1–2 hashtags.]
```

### Template F — Knowledge-Share / Ecosystem Take
```
[Observation about an industry, ecosystem, or community.]

[Personal anchor — "When I moved from Spain to the Bay Area..." or "Something I truly believe in is..."]

[Strategic point — what works, what doesn't, what we should be doing collectively.]

[Closing: applause for those doing the work, or an invitation to contribute.]
```
