## Tone Markers

- **Principled** — leads with a clear belief and walks the reader through the reasoning.
- **Technical** — names the actual mechanism (KV cache, canary deployments, observability), never hand-waves.
- **Sober** — explicitly separates signal from hype; resists overclaiming.
- **Builder** — frames work as practice, not performance ("we are not doing it as a ritual").
- **Reflective** — paragraphs feel like they were written slowly.
- **Direct** — short sentences, no hedging.
- **Quietly ambitious** — admits the problem is hard, then says he's excited to be working on it.

## Vocabulary

- **Core terms:** real-time, browser automation, LLM, KV cache, inference, latency, observability, canary deployments, SLA, agentic, scalability, throughput, quantization, hardest problems, edge of what's possible, shipping fast, extreme ownership, world-class team, category-defining.

  - Hashtag clusters (3+).

## Sentence Structure

- Short sentences predominate. Often 6–14 words.
- Frequent parallel-structure triplets — three short consecutive sentences that hammer one point:
  > `That is what they judge. / That is what they remember. / That is what they associate with Supersonik.`
- Paragraphs of 1–3 sentences. White space matters.
- Will use a small bulleted list (3–5 items) to enumerate technical or operational specifics.
- Often closes with a one-line takeaway sentence sitting alone.

### Hook (line 1)

Patterns observed:
- **Practice observation:** `We are reviewing real client calls with our AI agent at Supersonik.`

Never: bombastic announcements, "I'm thrilled to..." openers, vague AI hype.

### Body

- Engineering-culture posts: observation → why it's high leverage → principle → forward-looking note.
- Industry analysis posts: external news → what's happening underneath → what it changes for shipping → "the real shift."
- Technical paper posts: paper finding → why it matters technically → sober assessment of impact.
- Builder/launch posts: technical specifics of what was hard ("control a browser interactively, orchestrate LLMs, speak and understand multiple languages, respond in under one second").

### CTA (last line)

- Often a one-line distilled takeaway, no explicit CTA: `That is the real shift.` / `Less hype, more signal.`
- For hiring: `If you are interested in solving the hardest problems on real-time AI, join us!` / `DM me.`
- Almost never a product link CTA.

## Formatting Rules

- **No headers** inside posts.
- **Line breaks** between every 1–3 sentences. White space carries the rhythm.
- **Bullet lists** for technical/operational specifics (3–5 items). Dash-led, no emoji bullets.
- **Bold** never.
- **Emoji:** sparing and functional. 🚀 on launch posts. Rarely more than 1.
- **Hashtags:** 0–1. Almost never used in his recent voice.
- Post-length: short-to-medium (100–250 words). Comfortable with a 400-word origin/fundraise post when the moment warrants.

## Engagement Boosters

- **External news anchor + sober reframe** — best engagement format. Anthropic, Google Research, OpenAI releases re-cast with the "what actually changes" angle.
- **Parallel-structure triplet** for the rhetorical peak of a post.
- **Naming specific mechanisms** (KV cache, canary deployments) — signals depth, attracts technical comments.
- **One-line closing takeaway** — saveable, quotable.
- **Founder origin with technical specifics** (under-1-second response, browser control, multi-language) — outperforms generic "we raised" posts.

## Anti-patterns / What Not to Write

- Marketing-style announcements ("We're thrilled to announce..."). Joaquim owns gracious; Dani owns punchy founder-pride; Pol stays builder-substantive.
- Vague AI hype without a named mechanism.
- Long lists of bullet points without a thesis.
- Motivational filler ("Dream it, build it, ship it" — that's Dani's line, not his).
- Hashtag clusters.
- Pitching the product. Pol talks about how it's built, not what it can do for you.
- Performative engineering takes ("Hot take: junior devs should..."). He stays principled and grounded in what he and his team actually do.
