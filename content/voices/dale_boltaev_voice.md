## Tone Markers

- **Direct** — clear calls to action and straightforward descriptions.
- **Energetic** — frequent enthusiastic emojis (🍎, 🚀, 🇺🇸) and positive framing.
- **Celebratory** — never cynical, never problem-led. Always momentum-led.
- **Professional yet accessible** — uses industry terms ("AEs", "founding sales playbook") but maintains a conversational "let's speak" vibe.
- **First-person** — "I'm excited", "I'm recruiting", "Myself and X are…".
- **Personal-warm on personal posts** — travel, promotions, in-person events read warmer than hiring posts.

## Post Structure (LinkedIn)

### Hook (line 1)
A short hook — often a flag emoji combo, a milestone, or a single bold statement.

Examples:
- `🇺🇸🇺🇸🇺🇸`
- `HUGE NEWS 🔥`
- `2018: Deel was founded 🦄`
- `Supersonik is heading for the USA 🇺🇸`
- `We're hiring [ROLE] at Supersonik!`

Lead with one of: country-flag combo, single bold statement, number/milestone, or "I'm excited to…".

### Body
- 80–180 words.
- 1–2 sentences per "paragraph" (each on its own line).
- For Supersonik hiring: arrow bullets (`→`) for responsibilities and benefits.
- For Deel-era hiring: emoji bullets (`🚀`) for the "Why join?" section.

### CTA (last line)
Always present. One of:
- `Let's speak!`
- `Let's connect!`
- `Get in touch!`
- `Please get in touch for more info!`
- `DM me!`
Never ends flat.

## Formatting Rules

- **Headers inside posts:** no.
- **Line breaks:** after almost every sentence. Heavy.
- **Bold:** rarely. Used in big-news framing.
- **Emojis:** heavy but patterned. Rules:
  - 🚀 = growth, hiring, opportunity (most-used)
  - 🔥 = momentum, "huge news"
  - 💸 / 📈 = milestones, ARR, funding
  - Country flags (🇺🇸 🇬🇧 🇳🇱 🇫🇷 🇩🇪 🇵🇹 🇬🇹) = market/role geography (often 3 in a row at the top of geo posts)
  - 🦄 / 🍾 / 🎉 = company milestones, promotions
  - → arrow = bullets in Supersonik-era hiring posts
- **Hashtags:** rare and consistent. `#WorkFromAnywhere` is the staple.
- **Length:** under 180 words always.

## Platform Rules

- **LinkedIn:** energetic, recruitment-focused, heavy on emojis and bullet points. Default to image. Tag colleagues. See `../platform_adaptation/linkedin.md` for general rules.
- **Email / DMs:** short, personalized, focused on setting up a conversation. Match the "let's speak!" energy.

## Engagement Boosters

In order of observed lift:

1. **Personal authentic moments > company news > product features.** Promotion, joining a company, travel photos, in-person events all 3–10× the median.
2. **Image attached.** Beats both article-link and text-only by a wide margin.
3. **Tagging colleagues by name.** Every top-10 post tags 1–6 named people.
4. **Timeline / list format** ("2018: ... 2019: ... 2020: ...") with emoji per line drives shares.
5. **Country flag in the hook** (especially 3-flag combo) raises top-of-feed stop rate.
6. **Open-ended question at the close** ("What will tech sales look like in 5 years?") drives comments in Supersonik-era posts.

## Anti-patterns / What Not to Write

- Posts longer than 180 words.
- Paragraphs longer than 2 sentences.
- Posts without a CTA in the last 2 lines.
- Text-only when a visual is available.
- Hashtags beyond `#WorkFromAnywhere`.
- Corporate jargon (robotic tone).
- Multi-paragraph blocks (walls of text).
- Hiring or team posts without at least one named-person tag.
- Cynical or problem-led framing.
