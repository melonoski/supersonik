# Ben the RevOps Lead

T1 Implementator — owns the data layer, routing, scoring, attribution. Knows Clay or wants to. **The user, not the sponsor.**

## What they own

The plumbing. Demos route to the right rep, signals get scored, qualification fields populate, attribution closes the loop. If the data layer is broken, sales blames Ben.

## Pain

- Inbound flow is gnarly — demos route to the wrong rep, fall through cracks.
- Signals are scattered across Clay, Apollo, the website, the CRM, custom enrichment.
- Manual qualification eats AE time AND degrades data quality.

## What they read

GTM Engineering blog, Clay docs, Modern Sales Pros, RevOps Co-op Slack/community, the *Beyond the Inbox* podcast.

## What lands in copy

Technical detail wins. CRM integration, signal-based routing, "AI agent that writes back to your data layer." How fields map. What gets fired in what conditions. Architecture diagrams.

## Value prop (Supersonik → Ben)

Ben gets a routing-aware AI demo agent that writes back to his data layer cleanly — signals, conversation transcripts, qualification fields populated automatically. No more "demo happened somewhere, we'll find the notes later." The integration is the product.

## Trust devices

- Pol Ruiz's CUDA/inference engineering background — peer technical credibility.
- ElevenLabs / Clay case studies — companies running data-aware GTM stacks.

## Common objections + how to handle

- **"Will this integrate with our CRM?"**
  - Native writebacks to Salesforce + HubSpot. Conversation transcripts, qualification fields, routing signals.
- **"We don't want another tool."**
  - Replaces 2–3: demo-on-demand, live chat at peak intent, manual SE-notes-to-CRM step. Net tool count goes down.
- **"How do you handle our custom field model?"**
  - Map your fields once; agent writes to them on every conversation.
