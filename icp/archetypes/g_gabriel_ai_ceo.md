# Gabriel the AI-Native CEO

T3 Decision Maker — founder/CEO of an AI-native or dev-tool SaaS. Moves fast. Buys infrastructure instead of hiring. **Reference-value compounding** — landing one logo here buys credibility for the entire Tier 1 pipeline.

## What they own

The whole company. The GTM thesis. The bet. Reports to nobody except the board (and the cash runway).

## Pain

- Demand exploded faster than the team. Small team can't run a global demo motion.
- Would rather buy AI infrastructure than hire SDRs — culturally allergic to bloated sales orgs.
- Procurement on his side is fast — the friction is vendor-side (long sales cycles, custom integrations). He picks tools the way he picks libraries.

## What they read

Lenny's Newsletter, Stratechery, AI Twitter / X (intensely), a16z portfolio updates, Hacker News (HN), First Round Review.

## What lands in copy

Peer signal: "ElevenLabs runs demos this way; Lovable does X; you should too." Speed of pilot. AI-native architecture (not "we built AI features", but "this is AI-native from the inference layer up").

## Value prop (Supersonik → Gabriel)

Gabriel buys AI infrastructure instead of hiring SDRs. Supersonik IS the SDR-to-demo motion for an AI-native company. He moves fast (procurement isn't the blocker) and gets to talk to other founder-CEOs who already shipped this. Cultural fit > sales pitch.

## Common objections + how to handle

- **"We're product-led, not sales-led."**
  - Supersonik *is* product-led — the demo IS the product experience, just guided. No sales-led overhead added.
- **"Custom integration overhead?"**
  - 1–2 weeks max. Same effort as integrating Vercel. Script tag + product metadata.
- **"We don't run a demo motion at all."**
  - Then we're not the fit *today*. Stay in touch; demo bottleneck shows up when revenue scales past the founder-AE phase.
