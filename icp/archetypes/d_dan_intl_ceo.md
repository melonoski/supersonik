# Dan the International CEO

T2 Decision Maker — owns international revenue, time-to-pipeline in new markets, regional P&L.

## What they own

International revenue + the regional P&Ls underneath it. Time-to-pipeline in a new market is his top KPI. Reports to global CEO.

## Pain

- New markets need demo capacity *yesterday* — but hiring local SEs takes 6–9 months from req to ramp.
- Translation of demo collateral lags the sales motion. Marketing has the landing page, sales doesn't have the demo.
- Every regional VP is asking for SE hires he can't fund fast enough.

## What they read

International SaaS / cross-border GTM blogs, SaaStock newsletter, Bessemer cloud reports, Carta benchmarks, OnDeck's *International Operators*.

## What lands in copy

Time-to-pipeline-in-new-market math. "Multilingual coverage without local hiring." Specific compression of 6–9 months → days.

## Value prop (Supersonik → Dan)

Dan compresses time-to-pipeline in a new market from 6–9 months (hire SE → ramp → demos) to days. Supersonik runs demos in 70+ languages on day one of market entry. He gets regional VPs unblocked without funding 20 new SE hires.

## Trust devices

- Joaquim Lechà's Typeform experience — scaled a multilingual SaaS into dozens of countries.

## Common objections + how to handle

- **"Procurement will take 6 months."**
  - Run a paid pilot in one region first. SOC 2 + GDPR posture available for review.
- **"Our regional VPs want local headcount, not software."**
  - Not either/or. Software covers the demo bottleneck while you build the local team. Bridges the 6-month ramp gap.
- **"We tried localized demo content — quality drifted."**
  - That's the canonical Supersonik problem statement. The agent runs the *same* product narrative everywhere, just translated in real-time.
