# Eric the Enablement Lead

T2 Implementator — owns demo consistency across regions, rep productivity, certification programs.

## What they own

Sales enablement programs, demo standards, certification tracks, onboarding ramps, the content library. Every region's SEs report to a different VP but Eric owns whether they all tell the same product story.

## Pain

- Demo quality drifts across regions. The Madrid SE describes feature X one way; the Berlin SE describes it another way. Consistency is impossible at scale.
- Onboarding a new SE in a new geo takes 3+ months before they're demo-ready.
- Localized demos are an unsolved content-ops problem — translation lags, regional adaptations break the canonical narrative.

## What they read

Sales Enablement PRO, Highspot / Seismic / MindTickle blogs, SiriusDecisions / Forrester reports, the *Sales Productivity Podcast*.

## What lands in copy

"Demos that match the canonical product narrative everywhere." Onboarding-ramp compression. Specific drift metrics if you have them.

## Value prop (Supersonik → Eric)

Eric gets a single canonical demo experience that runs everywhere, in every language, with zero drift. No more "each region tells the product story differently." Onboarding ramp compresses because new SEs don't have to memorize the script — the agent owns it. Enablement effort moves from "teach every SE the demo" to "tune the agent once."

## Trust devices

- Enterprise SaaS case studies (peer enablement teams).
- Pol Ruiz's technical credibility — answers the "how does it stay on-script" question.

## LinkedIn signals to look for

| Signal | Source | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Recent SOP / enablement tooling discussion | LinkedIn posts | Topical interest |
| Hiring multiple SEs across regions | LinkedIn Jobs | Enablement load is rising |
| Recent CRM or enablement-platform announcements | Press, LinkedIn | Tool churn — open to a new fit |

## Common objections + how to handle

- **"Will the agent stay on-script?"**
  - It's grounded in your product. Every response is constrained to the demo narrative you approve. You audit the script; the agent doesn't improvise off it.
- **"Our enablement content is bespoke per region."**
  - Translation happens in real-time. You maintain one source of truth; the agent localizes. Net: less per-region content work, not more.
- **"How do we measure quality vs. our human SEs?"**
  - Side-by-side demo evaluation against your existing rubric — same one you use to certify SEs.

## Example signal-anchored opens (3)

1. **Hiring multiple SE regions** — "You're ramping SEs in DACH and Iberia simultaneously. The drift problem at that scale is brutal — happy to show how a single-source agent solves it."
2. **Enablement-platform churn signal** — "Saw [Company] just rolled out a new enablement platform. Demo-narrative drift is usually the next problem on the list."
3. **Recent SOP post** — "Caught your post on demo standardization. We're building the agent-side of that exact problem."
